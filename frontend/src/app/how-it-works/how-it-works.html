How it works

From idea to impact — with visual checkpoints

Four stages, each with clear inputs, platform checks, and outputs. Larger tiles highlight the most time‑intensive moments.

1

Draft

📝

Frame the problem, goals, affected groups, and early options. Use guided templates and cite sources inline.

Inputs
Problem outline, evidence links, budget rough‑cut
Platform checks
Identity verification, duplicate detection, basic legality scope
Outputs
Shareable draft with citations & impact checklist
Typical duration
1–7 days
2

Expert review

🧠

Independent domain experts annotate feasibility, risks, legal fit, and cost models.

Who
Rotating pool with disclosures; randomized assignment
Checks
Evidence validation, conflict‑of‑interest flags
Outputs
Plain‑language summary, risk register, budget estimate
Typical duration
3–10 days
3

Deliberation

🗳️

Structured debate with argument maps, amendments, and civility tools that keep discourse productive.

What you see
Argument map, trade‑offs, localized impact overlays
Signals
Reputation weighting, rate‑limits for noise, down‑ranking of uncivil posts
Outputs
Consolidated draft + set of vetted alternatives
Typical duration
5–14 days
4

Advance

📜

When thresholds are met, proposals move to a binding vote or legislative inbox with full transparency.

Gate
Support + review thresholds achieved
Outputs
Voting brief, implementation tracker if adopted
Aftercare
Post‑adoption audit and metrics reporting

Quick answers

Who can propose?

Any verified citizen. Organizations can co‑sponsor with disclosure.

How are experts chosen?

A rotating pool within domain expertise; assignments are randomized and disclosures are public.

What counts as support?

Weighted by verification and reputation to reward constructive participation.
